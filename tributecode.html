B. R. Ambedkar

A great Indian who made phenomenal contributions to India and its Constitution.

About the Legend

• Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, popularly known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, India.

• Ambedkar was a key architect of the Indian Constitution and served as the first Law and Justice Minister of independent India.

• He earned multiple doctorates from prestigious institutions like Columbia University and the London School of Economics, specializing in economics, law, and political science.

• As a leader of the Dalits (formerly known as untouchables), Ambedkar dedicated his life to fighting social discrimination and advocating for the rights of marginalized communities in India.

• Ambedkar was instrumental in the formation of the Indian Constitution, ensuring it enshrined fundamental rights and aimed to eliminate caste discrimination.

• In 1932, he played a significant role in negotiating the Poona Pact with Mahatma Gandhi, which provided reserved seats for Dalits in the provincial legislatures within the framework of a single Hindu electorate. • Ambedkar's scholarly works include 'Annihilation of Caste,' a groundbreaking critique of the caste system, and 'The Buddha and His Dhamma,' which outlines his conversion to Buddhism and its teachings.

• In 1956, Ambedkar embraced Buddhism along with millions of his followers, initiating the Dalit Buddhist Movement to escape the caste-based oppression in Hinduism.

• He served on various commissions and committees, notably contributing to labor reforms, economic planning, and social justice policies in India.

• Ambedkar was honored posthumously with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1990 for his contributions to the nation.

• His birthday, April 14, is celebrated as Ambedkar Jayanti and is a public holiday in India, marking his legacy and influence on Indian society.

• Ambedkar's contributions to women's rights, including advocating for gender equality and women’s education, are also widely recognized.

• Throughout his life, Ambedkar faced immense social and political challenges but remained steadfast in his mission to promote equality, justice, and human rights.

• Ambedkar passed away on December 6, 1956, in New Delhi, but his vision and efforts continue to inspire movements for social justice and equality in India and around the world.
